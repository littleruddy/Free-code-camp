Vigin Trains

Former operator of InterCity West Coast and CrossCountry UK Franchise.

Here's a time line Virgin Trains History:

  • Nov 1996 - Virgin Trains was awwarded the InterCity CrossCountry franchise.
  • 5 Jan 1997 -Virgin Trains CrossCountry start operations.
  • Jan 1997 - Virgin Trains was awwarded the InterCity West Coast franchise.
  • 9 Mar 1997 - Virgin Trains West Coast start operations.
  • Oct 1998 - Virgin Group sold 49% of the shares in the Virgin Rail Group to Stagecoach.
  • Oct 1998 - Virgin signed a deal to lease 44 8 & 9 Carriage class 390 Pendolinos built by Alstom
  • 2002 - Virgin opted to extend all Pendolinos to 9 cars.
  • Mar 2002 - Virgin signed deal with Porterbrook to rebuild 12 class 47's into 57/3's.
  • Jun 2002 - The first class 57/3 thunderbird was delivered.
  • July 2002 - Virgin CrossCountry and Virgin West Coast franchises suspended due to the inabitily of Railtrack and Network Rail to deliver 140mph West Coast Main Line upgrade.
  • Jan 2003 - First Pendolino entered service.
  • June 2005 - Final loco hauled trains were withdrawn
  • Sept 2004 - HST's withdrawn and replaced with 5 car voyagers, class 47's and class 90's hauled mk3 sets
  • Sept 2005 - Class 57/3's hauling Pendolinos replaced the loco hauled mk 3's
  • Sept 2006 - Department fot Transport announce shortlist for the new CrossCountry franchise with Virgin Rail Group included.
  • 23 Feb 2007 - Class 390 Pendolino "City of Glasgow" forming the 17:15 London Euston to Glasgow Central service derailed in the village of Grayrigg in Cumbria.
  • 10 July 2007 - Department For Transport awarden new CrossCountry contract to Arriva
  • 11 Nov 2007 - Virgin CrossCountry transfers to Arriva CrossCountry.
  • Dec 2008 - Loco hauled serviced replaced by 5 car voyagers.
  • 2009 - Richard Branson launched a campaign to have the next franchise period extended for 20 to 30 years, so that Virgin could spend more on infrastructure and see a return on its investment.
  • Jan 2011 - Department for Transport called for expressions of interest in bidding for the next InterCity West Coast franchise.
  • Mar 2011 - Department for Transport announce shortlist for franchise with Virgin Rail Group included.
  • May 2011 - Secretary of State for Transport announced that the end date had been postponed to allow the recommendations in the McNulty Report to be absorbed.
  • Oct 2011 - Department announced that Virgin had been granted a franchise extension until December 2012.
  • Jan 2012 - Invitation to tender issued to shortlisted bidders.
  • Mar 2012 - Virgin West Coast contract was due to expire.
  • Aug 2012 - Department for Transport awarded FirstGroup the new franchise.
  • Oct 2012 - The government announced that it was cancelling the franchise competition for the InterCity West Coast franchise after discovering significant flaws in the way the franchise process was conducted. The government announced that it would negotiate with Virgin Trains to run the InterCity West Coast franchise for a further 9 to 13 months.
  • Dec 2012 - Virgin was awarded a 23-month management contract to run the franchise until 9 November 2014
  • Dec 2012 - 31 Pendolinos were extended by 2 cars to 11 cars and 4 new sets were delivered.
  • Mar 2013 - ecretary of State announced that the franchise would again be extended until 31 March 2017.
  • July 2013 - Office of Rail Regulation rejected an application by Virgin Trains to operate new services to Shrewsbury and Blackpool North, citing capacity constraints on the West Coast Main Line.
  • Nov 2013 - a revised application for services to Shrewsbury and Blackpool North was approved
  • Dec 2014 - Services start running to Shrewsbury and Blackpool North.
  • Nov 2016 - Franchise extended until March 2019.
  • Mar 2018 - The West Coast contract would end in September 2019 when the new West Coast Partnership franchise would commence.
  • Dec 2018 - Announced Virgin Trains had been granted a one-year extension untill March 2020
  • Aug 2019 - Inter-city West Coast franchise awarded to Avanti West Coast
  • 7 Dec 2019 - Virgin Trains cease to operate trains. Final service was at 21:42 from London Euston to Wolverhapmton arriving at 23.45.
